Monthly Update - October

So these past few months have been pretty busy to say the least...

Over the past two months I've gone from enjoying my summer holidays and working a little to starting my first year of A Levels full time at college and playing basketball everyday in the academy too. College has been great so far - I'm studying Mathematics, Further Mathematics, Computer Science and Physics. It's great to be studying subjects I have genuine interest in every day and to keep progressing.

Something new that has happened is a massive growth in the contribution and community around bric - the text editor. With college and all starting I haven't really had the chance to maintain bric much and wasn't really planning on maintaining it after a while. But after all the new contributors I feel like I should start helping out a bit more as it will be a good experience to manage an open source project. There is definitely lots to do but I'm sure I can fit in small amounts here and there and let the community work their magic.

On other lines, I've started learning more about machine learning and AI. This is because I'd like to have my second year Computer Science project based on machine learning. I did a bit of searching and found a few free online books that I am going to work my way through, starting with Michael Nielsen's book. AI and machine learning is something I find super interesting and it also helps that it is the new buzz in the tech industry and some early experience wouldn't hurt at all.

So my current programming agenda looks something like this: 45% machine learning, 45% coding-interview-university (self teaching computer science knowledge) and 10% bric maintainence. Fitting all of this in along with college work and basketball will definitely be a challenge but it is all what I want to do, so I'm really making an effort to organise my time a lot better (obviously if I can't fit it all in college work will come first but hopefully I should be all good).

Anyway, just a quick update.
